1 AS 2021 61 SR 818.101

Section 1 Purpose and Subject Matter

Art. 1

1 This Ordinance is intended to prevent the spread of the Sars-Cov-2 coronavirus.

2 It regulates the following matters for persons who enter Switzerland from a country or region with an increased risk of infection:

a.
recording contact data;
b.
the requirement to quarantine and its enforcement;
c.
testing.

3 It further regulates recording the contact data of persons who enter Switzerland by rail, bus, ship or aircraft from a country or region that does not have an increased risk of infection.

Section 2 Countries and Regions with an Increased Risk of Infection

Art. 2

1 There is an increased risk of infection with Sars-CoV-2A in a country or region if at least one of the following requirements is satisfied:

a.
In the country or region concerned, a mutation of the Sars-Cov-2 coronavirus has been proven to exist that carries a higher risk of infection or causes a more severe form of the disease than the strain of the virus that is widespread in Switzerland.
b.
In the previous 14 days the number of new infections per 100 000 persons in the country or region concerned is more than 60 higher than in Switzerland, and this number is not due to specific incidents or cases occurring in a narrowly defined geographical area.
c.
The available information from the country or region concerned does not allow a reliable assessment to be made of the risk situation, and there are indications that there is an increased risk of transmission in the country or region concerned.
d.2
In the previous seven days there have been repeated instances of infected persons who have stayed in the country or region concerned entering Switzerland.

2 The lists of countries or areas with an increased risk of infection are set out in Annex 1.

Section 3 Recording Contact Data

Art. 3 Obligations for persons entering Switzerland

1 Persons entering Switzerland from a country or region with an increased risk of infection must provide in the following manner their contact data in accordance with Article 49 of the Epidemics Ordinance of 29 April 20154 before entry:

a.
online by means of the platform for recording contact data for travellers5 provided by the Federal Office of Public Health (FOPH); or
b.
on the paper contact forms provided by the FOPH.

1bis Persons who do not enter Switzerland as a passenger with a passenger transport operator pursuant to Article 4 and who record their contact details on a contact form must retain the form for 14 days.6

2 Persons entering Switzerland from countries or regions that do not have an increased risk of infection must provide their contact data online or on paper only if they are entering Switzerland by rail, bus, ship or aircraft.

3 The following persons are exempted from the obligations in paragraphs 1 and 2:

a.
persons entering from regions on the border with Switzerland that have close economic, social and cultural ties with Switzerland;
b.
persons who carry goods or persons across the border as part of their professional activity;
c.7
persons who enter Switzerland solely in order to transit the country and who intend and are able to travel directly to another country; this exemption does not apply to persons who transit Switzerland with a passenger transport operator under Article 4 and make a stopover while here;
d.8
persons who transit Switzerland with a passenger transport operator under Article 4 without making a stopover while here;
e.9
persons who have been vaccinated against Sars-CoV-2; the persons who are regarded as having been vaccinated and the duration for which the vaccination is valid is regulated in Annex 1a;
f.10
persons who have been infected with Sars-CoV-2 and who are regarded as having recovered; the duration of the exemption is regulated in Annex 1a.11
4 For persons entering Switzerland from countries or areas listed in Annex 1 number 2, the exemptions from the obligation to record contact data in accordance with paragraph 3 letters e and f do not apply.12

Art. 4 Obligations for passenger transport operators

1 Operators carrying passengers internationally shall ensure that passengers entering Switzerland provide their contact data in accordance with Article 3 paragraphs 1 and 2.

2 They shall send the contact data under Article 3 paragraph 1 letter b to the FOPH on request within 24 hours.

3 They shall retain the contact data for 14 days and thereafter destroy the data.

4 They shall send the FOPH on request within 48 hours lists of all cross-border train, bus or ship journeys or flights that they have scheduled for the following month.

5 They shall send the contact data under Article 3 paragraph 1 letter b and the lists under paragraph 4 via the platform for passenger transport operators13 provided by the FOPH.

Art. 6 Duties of the FOPH and the cantons

1 The FOPH shall ensure that the contact data is available in order to enforce the quarantine requirement under Article 7 and that the data is forwarded immediately to the cantons responsible for the persons entering Switzerland.

2 As soon as it is notified that a person infected with Sars-CoV-2 has entered Switzerland, it shall take the following measures:

a.
It shall request the passenger transport operator to provide the contact data recorded on paper relating to the persons who entered Switzerland with the person infected with Sars-CoV-2.
b.
It shall establish the identity of the persons who were in close contact with the person infected with Sars-CoV-2 on the basis of contact data recorded online and the contact data in accordance with letter a.
c.
It shall forward the processed contact data immediately to the cantons responsible for the persons entering Switzerland.

3 The FOPH may delegate its duties under paragraphs 1 and 2 to third parties. In doing so, it shall ensure that data protection and data security are guaranteed.

4 It shall destroy the data one month after the entry of the persons concerned.

5 The cantons shall destroy the data one month after they have received the same from the FOPH.

Section 4 Testing, Quarantine, and Notification Requirements for Persons entering Switzerland

Art. 7 Requirement to be tested and to quarantine

1 The following persons must prove that they have undergone a molecular-biological test for Sars-CoV-2 within the previous 72 hours and that the test result was negative:

a.
persons entering Switzerland who have at any time in the previous 10 days stayed in a country or region with an increased risk of infection;
b.
persons entering Switzerland on an aircraft from a country or region that does not have an increased risk of infection.

2 Persons under paragraph 1 letter a are required on entry to travel immediately and directly to their home or to other suitable accommodation. They must remain there without leaving at any time for 10 days following their entry (quarantine on entry).

3 Persons under paragraph 1 who are unable to produce proof of a negative test on entering Switzerland must immediately undergo one of the following tests:15

a.
a molecular-biological test for Sars-CoV-2; or
b.16
a Sars-CoV-2 rapid test according to the diagnostic standard.

4 Persons required to quarantine on entry may leave quarantine within 10 days provided they undergo either a molecular-biological analysis for Sars-CoV-2 or a Sars-CoV-2-rapid test that meets the diagnostic standard and the result is negative. The test may be carried out at the earliest on the seventh day of quarantine. The competent cantonal authority may, in justified cases, suspend the early termination of quarantine for persons entering from countries and regions listed in Annex 1 number 2.17

5 Persons who leave quarantine early pursuant to paragraph 4 must wear a face mask and maintain a distance of at least 1.5 metres from other persons when outside their home or accommodation until the point in time at which quarantine under paragraph 2 would have ended.

6 If a person has entered Switzerland from a country or region that does not have an increased risk of infection, the responsible cantonal authority may take account of the duration of the person’s stay in that country or region when calculating the time to be spent in quarantine under paragraph 2.

Art. 8 Exemptions from the requirement to be tested and to quarantine

1 The following persons are exempted from the requirement to be tested and to quarantine under Article 7:

a.
persons whose work in Switzerland is essential to maintain:
1.
the efficiency of the health service,
2.
public security and order,
3.
the efficiency of institutional beneficiaries as defined in Article 2 paragraph 1 of the Host State Act of 22 June 200718,
4.
Switzerland’s diplomatic and consular relations;
b.
persons who in the course of their professional activities transport passengers or goods across borders;
c.
persons who enter Switzerland for compelling professional or medical reasons and whose visit cannot be postponed;
d.
persons re-entering Switzerland after staying in a country or region with an increased risk of infection for compelling professional or medical reasons and whose visit could not have been postponed;
e.
persons who as transit passengers have stayed less than 24 hours in a country or region with an increased risk of infection;
f.
persons who enter Switzerland for the purpose of transiting the country and who intend and are able to travel on directly to another country;
g.
persons who re-enter Switzerland after participating in an event in a country or region with an increased risk of infection, provided they prove that their participation and stay took place in compliance with a specific precautionary measures plan; participating in an event includes in particular participating, normally in a professional capacity, in a sporting competition or cultural event, or attending a professional conference;
h.19
persons who prove that they have been vaccinated against Sars-CoV-2; the persons who are regarded as having been vaccinated and the duration for which the vaccination is valid is regulated in Annex 1a;
i.20
persons who can prove that they were infected with Sars-CoV-2 and that they have recovered; the duration of the exemption is regulated in Annex 1a;
j.21
persons under 16 years of age.

1bis Persons who can provide a medical certificate to prove that they are unable on medical grounds to provide the nasopharyngeal swab required for the Sars-CoV-2 test are also exempted from the requirement to be tested under Article 7.22

1ter When entering Switzerland from the countries and regions listed in Annex 1 number 2, the exemptions from the requirement to be tested and to quarantine under paragraph 1 letters c, d, g, h, i and j do not apply. Children under the age of 12 are however exempted from the requirement to be tested.23

2 Paragraph 1 does not apply to persons displaying symptoms of COVID-19, unless the person concerned can provide certified medical evidence that the symptoms have a different cause.

3 In the case of persons under paragraph 1 letter a, their employer shall verify and certify that entering Switzerland is essential.

4 The competent cantonal authority may in justified cases permit further exceptions from the requirement to be tested and to quarantine or relax certain conditions.

Art. 9 Notification requirement

Any person who is required to go into quarantine on entry in accordance with this Ordinance must notify the competent cantonal authority within two days of their arrival in Switzerland and follow the instructions they are given by this authority.

Art. 9a

1 Airlines must inform their passengers that they must be tested for Sars-CoV-2 before departure and that they will only be permitted to board the aircraft if they can produce a negative test result.

2 Airlines must verify before departure whether a negative test result is available that is based on a procedure that corresponds to the current state of the art in science and technology. In doing so, they must verify that:

a.
the sample for a molecular-biological analysis for Sars-CoV-2 was taken no more than 72 hours previously;
b.
the sample for an immunological Sars-CoV-2-rapid test was taken no more than 24 hours previously.

3 The document with the test result must contain the following information:

a.
the surname, first name and date of birth of the person tested;
b.
the date and time of taking the sample;
c.
the form of test pursuant to paragraph 2 letter a or b;
d.
the test result itself.

4 The airlines must not permit passengers who are unable to produce a negative test result under paragraph 2 to board the aircraft.

5 They may carry the following passengers without these passengers being required to produce a negative test result:

a.25
children and young persons under sixteen years of age;
b.
persons who can provide a medical certificate to prove that they must be transported to Switzerland as a matter of urgency on medical grounds;
c.
persons who have Swiss citizenship or hold a residence permit issued by Switzerland and who are unable to be tested for Sars-CoV-2 within a reasonable time or at reasonable expense; the persons concerned must sign a declaration confirming the lack of opportunity;
d.
persons who are using a Swiss airport while in transit without leaving that airport before continuing their journey;
e.26
persons who can prove that they have been vaccinated against Sars-CoV-2; the persons who are regarded as having been vaccinated and the duration for which the vaccination is valid is regulated in Annex 1a;
ebis.27
persons who can prove that they were infected with Sars-CoV-2 and that they have recovered; the duration of the exemption is regulated in Annex 1a;
f.
persons who can prove that they are unable on medical grounds to provide the nasopharyngeal swab required for the Sars-CoV-2 test.

6 When entering Switzerland from the countries and regions listed in Annex 1 number 2, the exemptions from the requirement to be tested under paragraph 1 letters a, e and ebis do not apply. Children under the age of 12 are however exempted from the requirement to be tested.28

Art. 9b

1 The Federal Department of Home Affairs shall update Annex 1 continuously in consultation with the Federal Department of Justice and Police, the Federal Department of Finance and the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs.

2 It shall update Annex 1a in accordance with the latest scientific knowledge and in consultation with the Federal Commission for Vaccination.

Section 5 Checks and Reports by Border Control Authorities

Art. 10

1 The border control authorities may conduct risk-based checks on persons entering Switzerland from countries or regions with an increased risk of infection. In doing so, they shall verify:

a.
that the person checked has tested negative in accordance with Article 7 paragraph 1;
b.
that the contact data of the person checked has been recorded in accordance with Article 3 paragraph 1.

2 If the person checked is unable to produce a negative test result or show that their contact data has been recorded, the border control authority shall report the matter to the responsible cantonal authority. The report shall include the details of the person checked, the time and place of the check, the address the person has specified in Switzerland, and the outcome of the check.

3 The border control authorities may impose fixed penalty fines.
